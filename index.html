Why Us

Keep Your Faith

This section highlights why our church community is the perfect place to grow spiritually, stay grounded in your faith, and experience God's love. It emphasizes our commitment to nurturing believers through sound teachings, heartfelt worship, and unwavering support on their faith journey.

At Shalom Assemblies of God, we are committed to nurturing your faith and spiritual growth. Our community provides a supportive environment to deepen your relationship with God, strengthen your trust in His promises, and walk in His purpose for your life.
Reference Verses:
"Let us hold unswervingly to the hope we profess, for He who promised is faithful." — Hebrews 10:23

Live by Faith

Living by faith means trusting in God's promises, even when our circumstances seem uncertain. It is a lifestyle of dependence on God and His Word, rather than what we see or feel.

"Now faith is confidence in what we hope for and assurance about what we do not see." — Hebrews 11:1

Love one another

Love is the hallmark of true discipleship. Jesus commands us to love one another, modeling His sacrificial and unconditional love.

"And now these three remain: faith, hope and love. But the greatest of these is love." — 1 Corinthians 13:13

Be courageous

"Have I not commanded you? Be strong and courageous. Do not be afraid; do not be discouraged, for the Lord your God will be with you wherever you go." — Joshua 1:9
Courage is not the absence of fear but trusting God despite it. Throughout Scripture, God reminds us to stand firm because He is with us. "The Lord is my light and my salvation—whom shall I fear?" — Psalm 27:1

Seal of God

The Seal of God marks His people as His own. It represents protection, ownership, and a call to holy living. Those who are sealed by God are identified by their faithfulness to Him and obedience to His word. "Do not grieve the Holy Spirit of God, with whom you were sealed for the day of redemption." — Ephesians 4:30 "Then I saw another angel... having the seal of the living God." — Revelation 7:2-3
